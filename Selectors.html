Global Warming

Global warming is the increase in the average temperature of the Earth's atmosphere and oceans over an extended period. This phenomenon is a significant part of climate change, which also includes shifts in weather patterns and other environmental changes


Causes

The primary driver of global warming is the increased concentration of greenhouse gases (GHGs) in the atmosphere, mainly due to human activities: Burning Fossil Fuels: The combustion of coal, oil, and natural gas for electricity generation, transportation, and industrial processes releases large amounts of carbon dioxide (CO₂). Deforestation: Trees absorb CO₂, so cutting them down reduces the Earth's capacity to remove these gases from the atmosphere. Industrial Activities and Agriculture: These sectors contribute to the release of potent greenhouse gases like methane (CH₄) and nitrous oxide (N₂O). The Greenhouse Effect: These increased GHGs trap heat from solar radiation in the atmosphere, warming the planet.


Effects

The warming of the Earth's surface leads to a range of impacts: Rising Sea Levels: Glaciers and ice sheets melt, contributing to a rise in global sea levels. Extreme Weather Events: There is an increase in the frequency and severity of heatwaves, as well as changes in precipitation patterns. Ecosystem Disruption: Changes in temperature and climate disrupt ecosystems and biodiversity. Threats to Food and Water Security: Shifts in climate patterns can threaten food supplies and the availability of fresh water.
